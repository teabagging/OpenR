Consequently, the area of triangle F_1MN is:boxed{S_{F_1MN} = dfrac{1}{2}|F_1M|cdot d = dfrac{1}{2} cdot dfrac{2sqrt{2}}{3} cdot 2sqrt{2} = dfrac{4}{3}}.3. Since vectors vec{F_1M} and vec{F_2N} are parallel, we can write lambda vec{F_1M} = vec{F_2N}. Given |vec{F_2N}| - |vec{F_1M}| = sqrt{6}, it follows that lambda > 1, and we can establish:lambda(x_1 + 2) = x_2 - 2, ; y_2 = lambda y_1, ; (x_2, y_2), (x_1, y_1) in C.Substituting these into the ellipse equation, x_2^2 + 2y_2^2 = 8, and solving for x_1 yields:x_1 = dfrac{1-3lambda}{lambda} = dfrac{1}{lambda} - 3.Consequently, we get y_1^2 = 4 - dfrac{(1-3λ)^2}{2lambda}, and hence |vec{F_1M}| = dfrac{λ+1}{sqrt{2}λ}. Solving the equation (λ-1) cdot dfrac{λ+1}{sqrt{2}λ} = sqrt{6} gives us lambda = 2 + sqrt{3} (the other root is discarded). Therefore, x_1 = -1 - sqrt{3}, and y_1 = dfrac{sqrt{3}-1}{sqrt{2}}. Thus, the slope k_{F_1M} = -dfrac{sqrt{2}}{2}, and the equation of the line F_2N is y = -dfrac{sqrt{2}}{2}(x - 2). Simplifying, we have the final line equation:boxed{x + sqrt{2}y - 2 = 0}.
